FAQs

Why a blockchain?

Blockchain technology offers a range of advantages that make it appealing for various applications and industries. Some of the key advantages of blockchain include:

  1. Decentralization: Blockchain operates on a decentralized network of nodes, reducing the need for a central authority or intermediary. This decentralization enhances security and reduces the risk of a single point of failure.

  2. Security: Transactions and data recorded on a blockchain are cryptographically secured, making it difficult for unauthorized parties to alter or manipulate the data. Once a transaction is added to the blockchain, it becomes extremely resistant to tampering.

  3. Transparency: Most blockchains are public or permissioned networks, meaning that transaction history is visible to all participants. This transparency increases trust among users and stakeholders.

  4. Immutability: Once data is recorded on the blockchain, it is nearly impossible to change. This immutability is particularly valuable for applications requiring a tamper-resistant ledger, such as financial transactions or legal contracts.

  5. Trustlessness: Blockchain enables trustless transactions, meaning parties can transact without needing to trust a central authority or intermediary. Trust is established through cryptographic and consensus mechanisms.

Why is this needed for space?
