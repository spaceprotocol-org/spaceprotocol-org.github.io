FAQs

How big is the space economy?

The space economy is poised for remarkable growth, with projections indicating that by 2030, it will be a  trillion-dollar industry . This exponential growth is fueled by an increasing number of satellites, with approximately  70,000 satellites anticipated to be in Low Earth Orbit (LEO) alone. As space technology continues to advance, the space economy is set to play a pivotal role in various sectors, including communication, Earth monitoring, and scientific exploration, among others.

Why is space asset coordination crucial?

With a rising number of satellites and space debris, a single collision can start a chain reaction, the  Kessler syndrome .  ESA estimates that there are 36,500+ space debris objects greater than 10 cm and 1 million+ objects from 1 cm to 10 cm currently in orbit. Even flakes of paint a few millimeters wide can cause significant damage due to their extremely high velocity (27000 km/hr).

Coordination, therefore, is vital to prevent collisions and protect the space environment. The World Economic Forum (WEF) has extensive  Space Debris Mitigation Guidelines  that encourage coordination to make space safer and sustainable for everyone.

What role can blockchain play in space safety and sustainability?

Blockchain ensures space safety and sustainability by enabling secure, decentralized tracking and real-time conjunction alerts. It fosters accurate predictions, informed decisions, and prevents risks associated with a single entity holding space object location information, reducing the potential for weaponization in conflicts.

How does blockchain help space operators?

Space operators benefit from blockchain solutions by securely coordinating space assets, eliminating the need to share actual accurate space asset locations while ensuring effective coordination. They gain free access to automated maneuver plans, adhering to  space sustainability guidelines which  can result in insurance benefits and safer, more efficient satellite missions.

As an individual, how can I contribute and get benefit?

As an individual, you can contribute and benefit by running a Verifier Node on our blockchain platform. Verifier nodes earn native coins through conjunction verification fees, akin to transaction fees in Bitcoin or gas fees in Ethereum. This ensures decentralization and the security of the network. Our commitment to being 'free and always will be' embodies our belief in an inclusive space for all and the principle of 'all for space,' where your participation helps shape a safer and more accessible space environment for everyone.
